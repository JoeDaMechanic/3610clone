Fundamentals of Digital Privacy

Encryption

Encryption is crucial for protecting data. It ensures that only authorized users can access information, keeping your personal and sensitive data secure from cyber threats.

Learn More About Encryption Tools

Secure Passwords & MFA

Strong passwords and multi-factor authentication (MFA) provide a robust defense against unauthorized access. Use unique passwords for each account and enable MFA for added security.

Secure Your Accounts Now

Data Backups

Follow the 3-2-1 rule to back up your data effectively: Keep three copies of your data, store it on two different media, and have one backup offsite. This ensures you are prepared for data loss scenarios.

Privacy Tools to Safeguard Your Digital Presence

VPN Usage

Use a VPN to secure your internet connection, especially on public Wi-Fi networks. VPNs encrypt your internet traffic, keeping your data safe from prying eyes and ensuring your online activities remain private.

Anonymized Emails & Browser Extensions

Utilize anonymized email addresses to protect your real email and prevent spam. Browser extensions like Facebook Container can help limit data tracking and enhance your privacy while browsing.

Auto-Updates

Enable automatic updates for your software and devices to keep them secure from vulnerabilities. Regular updates help patch security flaws and keep your systems protected from cyber threats.

What To Do When Things Go Wrong

Follow the steps to isolate affected systems, preserve evidence, and mitigate cyber incidents. Having a proper response plan helps minimize damage and aids in quicker recovery.

Reporting incidents through platforms like IC3 and FTC to help law enforcement track cybercrime. Reporting incidents promptly can help protect others and bring cybercriminals to justice.
